Verdict: No Duplication in Either Ecosystem

retrospective-provenance is the only skill that looks backward at completed work. All 20 LFA skills (including the 6 additions beyond obra) look forward. The LFA-only skills add depth to DURING-building (debugging techniques, test quality, validation layers) but none address post-completion analysis, provenance tracing, or ecosystem classification.

Conclusion

No Duplication in Either Ecosystem. Fills a Genuine Gap.

Across all 20 skills in the LFA marketplace (which is a superset of the 14 in obra/superpowers), there is zero duplication with retrospective-provenance.

The LFA additions made the DURING phase stronger (4 new skills) and the META phase stronger (2 new skills). But the AFTER-COMPLETION phase has zero coverage in both ecosystems. retrospective-provenance is the first skill to address: "Now that we've built something, how do we learn from the process?"

The 3 skills with LOW overlap (requesting-code-review, verification-before-completion, test-driven-development) are complementary — retrospective-provenance detects where those skills were or weren't used, creating a feedback loop that makes the entire ecosystem more effective.
